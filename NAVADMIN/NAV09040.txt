SECNAVINST 1730.7D, RELIGIOUS MINISTRY SUPPORT WITHIN THE DEPARTMENT OF 
THE NAVY.  REF B IS OPNAVINST 1730.1D, RELIGIOUS MINISTRIES IN THE NAVY.  
REF C IS MCO 1730.6D, COMMAND RELIGIOUS PROGRAMS IN THE MARINE CORPS.  
REF D IS COMDTINST M1730.4C, RELIGIOUS MINISTRIES WITHIN THE COAST 
GUARD. 
RMKS/1.  REF A ESTABLISHES THE CHIEF OF CHAPLAINS AS PROGRAM SPONSOR 
FOR THE PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT OF CHAPLAIN CORPS OFFICERS AND 
RELIGIOUS PROGRAM SPECIALISTS (RPS).  THE CHIEF OF CHAPLAINS CONDUCTS 
ANNUAL SHORT-COURSE TRAINING FOR CHAPLAIN CORPS OFFICERS AND RPS TO 
UPDATE ESSENTIAL TECHNIQUES AND PRACTICES IN MILITARY MINISTRY.  THIS 
TRAINING ENABLES CHAPLAINS TO OBTAIN CONTINUING EDUCATION UNITS 
REQUIRED TO MAINTAIN FAITH-GROUP CREDENTIALS.
2.  THE FY-09 PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT TRAINING COURSE (PDTC), "COMBAT 
OPERATIONAL STRESS CONTROL:  THE FAMILY DYNAMIC" WILL PROVIDE TRAINING 
TO CHAPLAINS, RPS, OTHER CAREGIVERS FROM NAVY MEDICINE, FLEET AND 
FAMILY SUPPORT CENTERS (FFSC), MARINE CORPS COMMUNITY SERVICES (MCCS), 
AND SENIOR SMALL UNIT LEADERS IN THE AREA OF PSYCHOLOGICAL RESILIENCE.  
TOPICS INCLUDE PRE-DEPLOYMENT FAMILY AND SUPPORT SYSTEM READINESS, 
"SUPPORTING THE SUPPORTERS" DURING DEPLOYMENT, AND THE SPECIAL NEEDS OF 
BEREAVED FAMILIES AND THOSE WHO ASSIST INJURED SERVICE MEMBERS.  IT 
WILL IMPART THE KNOWLEDGE AND SKILLS NECESSARY FOR CAREGIVERS AND 
LEADERS TO PROVIDE CROSS- FUNCTIONAL CARE.  
3.  FOR PERSONNEL FROM NAVY MEDICINE, FFSC AND MCCS.  CLINICAL 
CAREGIVERS WHO SUPPORT FAMILIES WITH DEPLOYMENT RELATED ISSUES ARE 
HIGHLY ENCOURAGED TO ATTEND.  CONTINUING MEDICAL EDUCATION (CME) UNITS 
ARE ANTICIPATED FOR MEDICAL PERSONNEL AND CONTINUING EDUCATIONAL UNITS 
(CEUS) ARE ANTICIPATED FOR CLINICAL LICENSED COUNSELORS.
