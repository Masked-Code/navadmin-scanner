Rmks/1.  Per ref (a), this NAVADMIN establishes the Chief of Chaplains as 
program sponsor for the professional development of Chaplain Corps officers 
and enlisted Religious Program Specialists (RP).  The Chief of Chaplains 
conducts annual short-course training for Chaplain Corps officers and RPs to 
update essential techniques and practices in military ministry.  This 
training enables chaplains to obtain continuing education units which may be 
required to maintain their credentials with their religious organizations.

2.  Per ref (b), the FY-14 Professional Development Training Course (PDTC) 
"Pastoral Care in Cases of Military Sexual Trauma" is required mission 
essential training for chaplains and RPs to provide effective religious 
ministry to those involved in circumstances of military sexual assault and to 
provide relevant advice to commanders as required by refs (a) and (b).  In 
addition to pastoral care competencies, the course includes an overview of 
the physiological and psychological impact of sexual trauma and a synopsis of 
the military justice process in circumstances of sexual assault.  Per ref 
(c), additional mandatory training covering guidance on benefits for same-sex 
spouses is included in day one of the PDTC.

4.  Per ref (d), Sexual Assault Prevention and Response training is mission 
essential to meet pre-deployment requirements for chaplains and RPs to ensure 
that counseling services needed by sexual assault victims and offenders in 
contingency and remote operations are met per ref (b).  Per refs (e) through 
(g), commands are authorized to provide appropriated funds in support of 
religious ministries within the command to include funding chaplains and RPs 
to attend annual PDTCs.

5.  Naval Chaplaincy School and Center (NCSC) uses the Enterprise Navy 
Training Reservation System to process course registration/reservations.  
Contact your unit, command, Navy Operational Support Center training office, 
department or Chaplain Corps program manager for guidance on how to request a

navigate to NCSC Navy Knowledge Online (NKO) community of practice at 
https://wwwa.nko.navy.mil/portal/navchapschool/home/admissions then click on 
professional development.  Registration is first come, first serve.
