RMKS/1.  This message announces education opportunities through the Center 
of Excellence in Logistics and Technology (LOGTECH) Institute for Defense and 
Business (IDB), hosted at the University of North Carolina (UNC), Chapel 
Hill during fiscal year 2024 (FY24).  LOGTECH enables and energizes 
participants to improve operations and accelerate adoption of future 
logistics capabilities throughout their organizations.  Partnering with the 
Kenan-Flagler Business School at UNC-Chapel Hill, LOGTECH offers two distinct 
education courses for military, DoD, and private sector personnel: 
  a.  LOGTECH Executive Course.  This course is designed for military Flag 
Officers and Senior Executive Service members to focus on strategic and 
organizational considerations from the senior executive perspective. 
 
FY24 Executive Course schedule:

  b.  LOGTECH Advanced Course.  This course is designed for CWO-4 to CWO-5, 
O-4 to O-6 military officers, and E-9 military personnel, as well as GS-13 to 
GS-15 civilians.  It explores leading-edge logistics technologies to prepare 
logisticians for the future.  FY24 Advanced Course schedule:

  c. Submission Requirements.  To obtain LOGTECH Advanced or Executive 
Course quotas, applicants must submit a standard Navy biography and signed 
SF-182 to their appropriate Echelon 2 liaison.  Advanced course applicants 
must also include a logistics problem statement for exploration during the 
course.  A sample problem statement is found in reference (a).  The Office of 
Chief of Naval Operations (OPNAV), Fleet Readiness and Logistics (N4) FY24 
LOGTECH course managers are: 
    (1) CDR Nol Koenig, OPNAV N4L5P, noel.k.koenig.mil@us.navy.mil 
    (2) LCDR Dartanyon King, OPNAV N4L5C, dartanyon.r.king.mil@us.navy.mil 
    (3) Ms. Olivia Burns, OPNAV N4L5H, olivia.t.burns2.ctr@us.navy.mil 
  d.  Selection criteria.  LOGTECH is a competitive program.  Selections are 
based on the following criteria: candidate qualifications (specifically 
career field and paygrade), package completeness (biography, SF-182, and 
problem statement), and problem statement quality. 
  e.  Additional Information.

  2. Funding.  Tuition, travel, lodging, and partial per diem are centrally 
funded in FY24.  Commands are responsible for funding per diem on the days of 
travel only.  Services, Agencies, and Combatant Commands also have the option 
to self-fund seats for any of the above classes. Should a command decide to 
self-fund, they will work directly with IDB after coordinating with OPNAV and 
appropriate Echelon II Point of Contacts.  Program cost is $9K per attendee 
and covers room, board, and tuition.
