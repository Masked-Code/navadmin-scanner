RMKS/1.  Per reference (a), nominations are requested for the annual Admiral 
Stan Arthur Awards.  Submit all nominations, not to exceed two pages, using 
OPNAVINST 5305/8 (Rev Oct 2013), at 
https://navalforms.documentservices.dla.mil/formsDir/_OPNAV_5305_8_4921.pdf.
Categories are:
    a.  Military Logistician of the Year
    b.  Civilian Logistician of the Year
    c.  Logistics Team(s) of the Year

2.  In line with the Navy's Design for Maintaining Maritime Superiority, and 
emphasis on innovation and high velocity learning, the Admiral Stan Arthur 
Award will recognize Navy military and civilian individuals and teams whose 
contributions and suggestions during Calendar Year 2018 have:
    a.  Enhanced the combat capability of the operating forces
    b.  Advanced technology, products, services and/or processes
    c.  Resolved major logistics issues
    d.  Developed innovative logistics that merit adoption for standard use
    e.  Had a positive effect on Joint operations
    f.  Advanced CNOs guidance for calendar year 2018

3.  Nominees may be involved in any and all phases of Navy acquisition, 
operational logistics, and Joint Services logistics, from early life-cycle 
planning to in-service logistics support.  Nominees may come from a variety 
of communities, to include, but not limited to:  civil engineering, supply, 
medical, fuel, ordnance and maintenance.  Contractors may be included on team 
award submissions, but will not receive monetary awards.

4.  Individuals and team(s) selected to receive the Admiral Stan Arthur Award 
for calendar year 2018 will be announced via Naval message.

5.  Award selectees receive the following:
    a.  Plaque and certificate for individual category and team(s)
    b.  $5,000 for Military Logistician of the Year
    c.  $5,000 for Civilian Logistician of the Year
    d.  $10,000 for the winning team to share among its military and civilian 
members

6.  Submit nominations via email, through Echelon II Command or Joint Command 
Headquarters to Director, Supply, Ordnance and Logistics Division (OPNAV N41) 
NLT COB Monday, 8 April 2019.  Echelon II commands may submit a total of 
three nominations, no more than one per award category.  Joint Command 
Headquarters may submit one naval nominee in each award category and one team 
nomination.  The joint team nomination may be comprised of non-naval military 
personnel if the Navy has administrative and support responsibility for the 
joint activity.  To receive confirmation of package arrival, nomination 
package must include e-mail address and phone number of Echelon II Command or 
Joint Command Headquarters POC.  Packages for individual military and 
civilian nominees must include a one-page biography.

7.  Released by VADM D. R. Smith, N4.//
