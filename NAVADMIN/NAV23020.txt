RMKS/1.  Per reference (a), nominations are requested for the annual Admiral 
Stan Arthur Awards.  Submit all nominations, not to exceed two pages, using 
OPNAVINST 5305/8 (Rev. Jan 2023), which is available at  
https://forms.documentservices.dla.mil/. 
  Categories are: 
    a.Military Officer Logistics and Sustainment Professional of the Year 
    b.Military Enlisted Logistics and Sustainment Professional of the Year 
    c.Senior Civilian Logistics and Sustainment Professional of the Year 
    d.Junior Civilian Logistics and Sustainment Professional of the Year 
    e. Logistics and Sustainment Team(s) of the Year 
 
2.  In line with the Chief of Naval Operations'(CNO) Navigation Plan 2022, 
the Admiral Stan Arthur Award criteria reflects an emphasis on delivering a 
more ready, lethal, better-connected, larger, and hybrid Fleet.  The Admiral 
Stan Arthur Award will recognize Navy military and civilian individuals and 
team(s) whose contributions and suggestions during Calendar Year 2022 have: 
    a.  Demonstrably enhanced the combat capability of the operating forces, 
    b.  Advanced technology, products, services and/or processes, 
    c.  Resolved major logistics issues, 
    d.  Developed innovative logistics that merit adoption for standard use, 
    e.  Had a positive effect on Joint operations, and/or 
    f.  Advanced CNO's guidance for calendar year 2022. 
 
3.  Nominees may be involved in any and all phases of Navy acquisition, 
operational logistics, and Joint Services logistics, from early life-cycle 
planning to in-service logistics and sustainment support.  Nominees may come 
from a variety of communities, to include, but not limited to:  civil 
engineering, supply, medical, fuel, ordnance and maintenance.  Contractors 
may be included on team award submissions, but will not receive monetary 
awards.  When completing OPNAV 5305/8, commands will separately address each 
criteria category listed above and specifically address achievements within 
those categories.  For any additional types of logistics or sustainment 
achievements, command shall identify additional categories of achievements 
and corresponding benefits. 
 
4.  Individuals and team(s) selected to receive the Admiral Stan Arthur Award 
for calendar year 2022 will be announced via naval message. 
 
5.  Award selectees receive the following: 
    a.  Plaque and certificate for individual category and team(s) 
    b.  $7,500 for Military Officer Professional of the Year 
    c.  $7,500 for Military Enlisted Professional of the Year 
    d.  $7,500 for Senior Civilian Professional of the Year (GS-13 to GS-15) 
    e.  $7,500 for Junior Civilian Professional of the Year (GS-12 and Below) 
    d.  $10,000 for the winning team to share among its military and 
    civilian members 
 
6.  Submit nominations in response to Enterprise Task Management Software 
Solution (ETMS2) tasker, or via e-mail if ETMS2 is unavailable, through 
echelon 2 command or United States Joint Forces Command (USJFCOM) to 
Director, Logistics Division (OPNAV N4L) no later than COB Friday, 31 March 
2023.  Echelon II commands may submit a total of three nominations, no more 
than one per award category.  Joint Command Headquarters may submit one naval 
nominee in each award category and one team nomination.  The joint team 
nomination may be comprised of non-naval military personnel if the Navy has 
administrative and support responsibility for the joint activity.  To receive 
confirmation of package arrival, nomination package must include e-mail 
address and phone number of echelon 2 command or USJFCOM point of 
contact.  Commands shall not submit nominee biographies or photos.  Any 
biographies and photos will not be included in the package reviewed by the 
awards panel.  Commands shall refrain from using gender based pronouns 
(he/she, his/her) and titles (Mr./Ms.) to ensure objectivity during award 
package review.  For individual awards, Nominee may be utilized to refer to 
the nominee, as necessary.
