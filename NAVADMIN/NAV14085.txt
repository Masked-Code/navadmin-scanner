SUBJ/FY-15 LAW EDUCATION PROGRAM SELECTION BOARD// 

REF/A/DOC/SECNAV/6SEP2002//
AMPN/REF A IS SECNAVINST 1520.7F, LAW EDUCATION PROGRAM.// 

RMKS/1.  This NAVADMIN announces convening of the FY-15 Law Education Program 
(LEP) Selection Board in February 2015.  Applications for this board must be 
received NLT 26 September 2014. Ref (a) provides information about LEP and 
application procedures.  Where information differs between this NAVADMIN and 
ref (a), this NAVADMIN takes precedence. 

2.  Participants must begin law school utilizing LEP orders NLT September 
2015 and prior to the sixth anniversary of their active duty commencement 
date.  This active duty time includes any enlisted service.  Officers whose 
sixth anniversary occurs prior to September 2015 must ensure they apply only 
to schools which permit starting the Juris Doctor course of study prior to 
their anniversary date.

3.  Per ref (a), eligible officers who desire to apply for the FY-15 LEP must 
submit a written request with current contact information and official 
college transcripts from each school attended NLT 26 September 2014, via 
their commanding officer to: 

       Center for Personal and Professional Development (CPPD)
       Voluntary Education Detachment
       Attn:  Code N2A2 LEP
       6490 Saufley Field Road
       Pensacola, FL 32509-5204
 
4.  An applicant's Law School Admission Test (LSAT) score report must be 
received prior to convening of the selection board in February 2015, but may 
be submitted via separate correspondence.  Website screenshots of LSAT scores 
will not be accepted.

5.  Applicants are required to contact PERS-4416E to schedule an interview 
with a senior (O5 or O6) active duty Judge Advocate General's Corps Officer. 

6.  Officer Community Managers (OCM) will identify designators and year 
groups that are eligible to transfer a limited number of active duty officers 
from their current community into the Judge Advocate General's Corps via LEP.  
The OCM will ensure that all candidates meet eligibility guidelines prior to 
releasing the applications to the board. 

7.  Point of contact for application procedures is Ms. Billie Colonna, CPPD, 
at (850) 473-6061/DSN 753 or via e-mail at billie.colonna(at)navy.mil.  For 
all other program questions contact LCDR Geraldo Padilla, PERS-4416E, (901) 
874-4084/DSN 882 or via e-mail at geraldo.padilla(at)navy.mil; or Ms. Jana 
Rittman, PERS-4416I, (901) 874-4087/DSN 882 or via e-mail at 
jana.rittman(at)navy.mil.

8.  This message is cancelled for record purposes on 30 September 2015.

9.  Released by Vice Admiral W. F. Moran, N1.//
