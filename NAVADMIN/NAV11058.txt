SUBJ/2011 ASSOCIATION OF NAVAL SERVICES OFFICERS CONFERENCE//

RMKS/1.  THIS NAVADMIN ANNOUNCES THE ASSOCIATION OF NAVAL SERVICES 
OFFICERS (ANSO) PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT AND TRAINING CONFERENCE BEING 
HELD 2-6 MAY 2011 AT THE OMNI SAN ANTONIO HOTEL AT THE COLONNADE, SAN 
ANTONIO, TX.

2.  ANSO IS THE LARGEST AND FASTEST GROWING ORGANIZATION TO FOSTER THE 
ADVANCEMENT AND RECOGNITION OF HISPANIC OFFICERS IN THE UNIFORMED NAVAL 
SERVICES.  ANSO IS ENDORSED BY ADMIRAL GARY ROUGHEAD, THE CHIEF OF NAVAL 
OPERATIONS (CNO), GENERAL JAMES F. AMOS, THE COMMANDANT OF THE MARINE 
CORPS (CMC), ADMIRAL ROBERT J. PAPP JR., THE COMMANDANT OF THE COAST 
GUARD (CCG), AND THE US MERCHANT MARINE SERVICES.  ANTICIPATED GUEST 
SPEAKERS ARE THE CNO, CMC, CCG, MASTER CHIEF PETTY OFFICER OF THE NAVY 
RICK D. WEST, AND SERGEANT MAJOR OF THE MARINE CORPS CARLTON W. KENT.  
THE 2011 CONFERENCE THEME IS "ANSO:  REMEMBERING THE PAST AND FORGING 
THE FUTURE!"

3.  UNIFORM FOR NAVY PERSONNEL E7 AND ABOVE WILL BE SERVICE KHAKI, AND 
SERVICE UNIFORM FOR E6 AND BELOW.  CIVILIANS SHOULD WEAR EQUIVALENT 
BUSINESS ATTIRE.  THE UNIFORM FOR THE FORMAL AWARDS BANQUET WILL BE 
DINNER DRESS WHITE JACKET (OR OPTIONAL DINNER DRESS WHITE UNIFORM FOR O3 
AND BELOW) AND BLACK TIE (OR EQUIVALENT) FOR CIVILIANS AND GUESTS.

4.  COMMANDING OFFICERS ARE AUTHORIZED TO SUPPORT ATTENDANCE.  
COMMANDING OFFICERS MAY AUTHORIZE AND ISSUE TAD ORDERS, TO INCLUDE 
REGISTRATION.

5.  REGISTRATION, TRANSPORTATION, AND LODGING INFORMATION CAN BE FOUND 
ON THE ANSO WEBSITE AT WWW.ANSOMIL.ORG.  CONFERENCE FEES ARE $325 FOR 
LIFE MEMBERS, $350 FOR MEMBERS, AND $375 FOR NON-MEMBERS.  AN ADDITIONAL 
$50 LATE FEE WILL BE APPLIED FOR ATTENDEES WHO REGISTER AFTER 22 APRIL 
2011.  NAVY PERSONNEL INTERESTED IN JOINING, REGISTERING, OR OBTAINING 
FURTHER INFORMATION MAY VISIT THE ANSO WEBSITE AT WWW.ANSO.ORG.  THE 
CHIEF OF NAVAL PERSONNEL WILL FUND THE FIRST 100 CONFERENCE 
REGISTRATIONS FOR THE RANKS OF LIEUTENANT COMMANDER OR BELOW.  EARLY 
CONFERENCE REGISTRATION AND ROOM RESERVATIONS ARE HIGHLY ENCOURAGED.
