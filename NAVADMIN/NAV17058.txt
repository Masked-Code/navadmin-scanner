SUBJ/ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION READINESS REVIEW SCHEDULE AND REQUEST FOR

REF/A/DOC/BUPERS/2NOV07//

AMPN/REF A IS BUPERSINST 1430.16F, ADVANCEMENT MANUAL FOR ENLISTED PERSONNEL 
OF THE U.S. NAVY AND U.S. NAVY RESERVE.

RMKS/1.  This NAVADMIN announces the schedule for Advancement Examination 
Readiness Reviews (AERR) from April to September 2017.

2.  In line with reference (a), this NAVADMIN should be read in its entirety.  
AERR events provide an opportunity for selected rating subject matter experts 
from fleet and shore-based commands to impact sailorization by affecting the 
careers of every test taker in the Navy.  Selected chief, senior chief, and 
master chief petty officers on active duty, full-time support and reservists 
currently on active duty for special work may take part in the process that 
enables the Navy to advance the most qualified and rating- knowledgeable 
Sailors.

3.  The AERRs are held at Naval Education and Training Professional 
Development Center (NETPDC) in Saufley Field, Pensacola, FL.
Temporary additional travel (TAD) in support of AERR events is considered 
mission critical and is flag approved.  All costs pertaining to TAD are 
funded by NETPDC.

4.  The following ratings are scheduled for AERR events between April and 
September 2017:  ABF, AC, AD, AG, AM, AME, AO, AS, ATI, AWF, AWR, AWS, AWV, 
AZ, BM, CM, CTI, CTR, DC, EA, EMN, EN, ET, ETR, ETV, FC, GM, HM, IC, IS, LS, 
MA, MM, MMA, MMN, MN, MR, ND, PS, QM, SO, SW and YN.

5.  To take part in the AERR process, visit 
https://www.nko.navy.mil/group/navy-advancement-center/mycpo.  This link 
provides the scheduled dates for the ratings listed above, eligibility and 
nomination package requirements, and other pertinent information regarding 
AERR events.

6.  Point of contact is Ms. Cheryl Dengler, NETPDC N33A, who can be reached 
at (850) 473-6169/DSN 753 or via e-mail at cheryl.dengler(at)navy.mil.

7.  Released by Vice Admiral R. P. Burke, N1.//
