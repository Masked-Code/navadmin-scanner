SUBJ/JOINT PROFESSIONAL MILITARY EDUCATION FOR SENIOR ENLISTED 
LEADERS// 
RMKS/1.  THIS NAVADMIN ANNOUNCES REQUIREMENTS AND SOLICITS APPLICATIONS 
FROM COMMAND MASTER CHIEFS FOR RESIDENT AND NON-RESIDENT JOINT P 
ROFESSIONAL MILITARY EDUCATION (JPME) OPPORTUNITIES.  THE MASTER CHIEF 
PETTY OFFICER OF THE NAVY (MCPON), IN PARTNERSHIP WITH THE NAVAL WAR 
COLLEGE (NWC), IS SPONSORING THIS INITIATIVE.  THE DEVELOPMENT OF JOINT 
LEADERS IS VITAL TO OUR NAVY'S ABILITY TO EXECUTE THE WIDE RANGE OF 
MILITARY OPERATIONS THAT SUPPORT OUR NATION'S STRATEGIC OBJECTIVES.  
EXPERIENCE IS NOT ENOUGH TO SUCCEED IN THE JOINT HEADQUARTERS AND JOINT 
COMBATANT COMMANDER ENVIRONMENT AND OUR NAVY SEEKS TO DEVELOP FULLY 
QUALIFIED AND INHERENTLY JOINT LEADERS AMONG ITS CADRE OF SENIOR 
ENLISTED SAILORS IN THE SAME MANNER THAT IT DEVELOPS OFFICERS WHO ARE 
SKILLED JOINT WARFIGHTERS AND STRATEGICALLY-MINDED, CRITICAL THINKERS.
2.  EACH NWC JPME PROGRAM CONSISTS OF THREE DISTINCT COURSES:
STRATEGY AND POLICY (S&P), NATIONAL SECURITY DECISION MAKING (NSDM), 
AND JOINT MILITARY OPERATIONS (JMO).  PROGRAM SPECIFICS, ELIGIBILITY 
AND APPLICATION PROCEDURES ARE LISTED BELOW:
    A.  RESIDENT PROGRAM AT THE NAVAL WAR COLLEGE:
        (1) PROGRAM SPECIFICS:
- RESIDENT SENIOR SERVICE COLLEGE CONVENES AT NWC IN NEWPORT, RI IN
  AUGUST AND NOVEMBER 2007 AND MARCH 2008.  THERE ARE TWO CMC QUOTAS
  FOR AUGUST AND ONE EACH FOR NOVEMBER 2007 AND MARCH 2008.  THIS
  FULL-TIME PCS, 10 TO 12-MONTH PROGRAM CONFERS A MASTER OF ARTS
  DEGREE IN NATIONAL SECURITY AND STRATEGIC STUDIES.
- PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS WILL SERVE AT LEAST ONE TOUR IN A WORLD-WIDE
  ASSIGNABLE BILLET UPON P
ROGRAM COMPLETION.  PARTICIPANTS INCUR AN
  ACTIVE DUTY OBLIGATION OF TWO YEARS, COMMENCING UPON COMPLETION OF
  (OR WITHDRAWAL FROM) THE COURSE OF INSTRUCTION.  THIS OBLIGATION
  IS DISCHARGED CONCURRENTLY WITH ANY OTHER SERVICE OBLIGATION THAT
  PROGRAM PARTICIPANTS MAY HAVE ALREADY INCURRED.  THIS AGREEMENT
  DOES NOT OBLIGATE THE NAVY TO RETAIN THE MEMBER ON ACTIVE DUTY.
        (2) ELIGIBILITY:
- POSSESS A BACHELOR'S DEGREE OR HIGHER FROM A REGIONALLY-ACCREDITED
  U.S. COLLEGE OR UNIVERSITY.  INDIVIDUALS SHOULD HAVE DEMONSTRATED
  ACADEMIC ACHIEVEMENT IN A CHALLENGING CURRICULUM.
- COMPLETED AT LEAST ONE CMC TOUR (OR 30 MONTHS OF FIRST CMC TOUR).
- HAVE COMPLETED NO MORE THAN 24 YEARS OF ACTIVE DUTY AS OF CLASS
  CONVENING DATE.
- BE A U.S. CITIZEN AND ELIGIBLE FOR A SECRET CLEARANCE.
- POSSESS BOTH THE ABILITY AND A STRONG DESIRE TO SERVE IN SENIOR
  ENLISTED LEADERSHIP POSITIONS AT MAJOR JOINT, MULTINATIONAL, AND
  NAVY COMMANDS (BOTH ASHORE AND AFLOAT).
       (3) APPLICATION PROCEDURES:
- INTERESTED CMCS MUST FORWARD REQUESTS FOR CONSIDERATION BY LETTER
  TO MCPON PER THE APPLICATION PROCEDURES LISTED BELOW.  THE
  MEMBER'S COMMANDER/COMMANDING OFFICER AND THE MEMBER'S FORCE/
  FLEET MASTER CHIEF MUST ENDORSE REQUESTS.  ALTHOUGH LETTERS ARE TO
  BE ADDRESSED TO MCPON, PACKAGES SHOULD BE MAILED TO:
    PRESIDENT, NAVAL WAR COLLEGE
    ATTN:  COMMAND MASTER CHIEF
    686 CUSHING ROAD
    NEWPORT, RI  02841-1207

  NOV CLASS; AND 19 JAN 08 FOR MARCH 2008 CLASS.  APPLICANTS WILL BE
  INDIVIDUALLY NOTIFIED OF THE RESULTS.
- SUBMISSION PACKAGES MUST INCLUDE THE FOLLOWING:
--COVER LETTER FROM MEMBER TO MCPON VIA CHAIN OF COMMAND ADDRESSING
  THE BELOW LISTED ELEMENTS (NOTE:  COVER LETTER WILL BE USED TO
