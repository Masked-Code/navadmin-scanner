RMKS/1.  The Chief of Naval Operations is pleased to announce the Annual Navy 
Intelligence Fellowship Program Opportunity for 2022-2023.  The Central 
Intelligence Agency (CIA) will host the 12-month program to provide the Navy 
fellow with an opportunity to work with the CIA Office of the Associate 
Director for Military Affairs, which coordinates, plans, executes and 
sustains worldwide activities that serve as the interface between the CIA and 
Department of Defense (DoD), based on priorities established by the Director, 
CIA to 
achieve national security objectives. 
 
2.  This program is open to Information Warfare (IW) officers in the 
cryptologic (1810) and intelligence (1830) warfare designators to enhance 
Navy IW exposure to national capabilities and organizations.  Navy military 
captains and commanders must have the following qualifications:  Masters 
degree, post-milestone or post-command O5, or any O6, and Joint Professional 
Military Education Phase I prior to being selected, or on schedule to 
complete by the end of this program.  Reference (a) provides a sample 
application package, sample curriculum vitae, service obligation, general 
utilization tour requirements, and additional eligibility requirements, which 
are available on the Department of the Navy issuances website 
(https://www.secnav.navy.mil/doni/default.aspx). 
 
3.  Applications are due 30 September 2021.  Send completed applications via 
encrypted e-mail to both the Naval Intelligence Activity point of contact 
listed above and the candidates detailer.  If unable to send encrypted e-
mail, utilize the DoD Safe Web Application at https://safe.apps.mil/.  
Hard copy applications will not be accepted. 
 
4.  Released by VADM Jeffrey E. Trussler, Deputy Chief of Naval Operations 
for Information Warfare, OPNAV N2N6.//
