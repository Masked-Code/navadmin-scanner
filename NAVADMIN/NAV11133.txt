SUBJ/SEPTEMBER 2011 (CYCLE 212) NAVY-WIDE PETTY OFFICER (E4-E6) ADVANCEMENT 
EXAMINATIONS FOR USN, FULL TIME SUPPORT (FTS), AND RESERVE CANVASSER RECRUITER 
(CANREC) SAILORS//

RMKS/1.  THIS NAVADMIN ANNOUNCES THE SEPTEMBER 2011 (CYCLE 212) NAVY-WIDE 
ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION FOR ADVANCEMENT IN RATE OF USN, FTS, AND RESERVE 
CANREC SAILORS.  THE EXAMINATION DATES ARE:
     E6 - 1 SEP 2011
     E5 - 8 SEP 2011
     E4 - 15 SEP 2011
     A.  REFER TO REF A REGARDING ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION
     OPTIONS
FOR SAILORS WHO ARE PENDING DEPLOYMENT TO, OR ARE DEPLOYED AND OPERATING IN, 
IRAQ, AFGHANISTAN, OR THE HORN OF AFRICA.
ADDITIONALLY, IF CYCLE 212 IS THE FIRST ADVANCEMENT CYCLE IN WHICH A 
REDEPLOYED SAILOR IS PARTICIPATING UPON COMPLETION OF A TOUR TO A THEATER 
COVERED BY THIS POLICY, EDUCATIONAL SERVICES OFFICERS
(ESO) MUST FOLLOW PROCEDURES IN REFS A AND REF B TO EXECUTE PROCESSING OF 
MISSED EXAMINATION CYCLES.  AFFECTED REDEPLOYED SAILORS MUST BE IDENTIFIED IN 
A SEPARATE ENCLOSURE TO THE LETTER OF TRANSMITTAL AS DIRECTED IN REF B, PARA 
614.
     B.  DO NOT ADMINISTER ANY CYCLE 212 EXAMINATIONS PRIOR TO
     THE
SCHEDULED EXAMINATION DATE UNLESS PROVIDED PER REFS A OR REF B, OR 
SPECIFICALLY AUTHORIZED BY NAVY PERSONNEL COMMAND PERS-812.
     C.  MUSICIAN (MU) SECOND CLASS PETTY OFFICERS WILL TAKE THE REGULAR MU1 
PAPER EXAMINATION ON 1 SEP 2011 AND WILL USE A DIGITAL MEDIA PLAYER PROVIDED 
BY THE NAVY EDUCATION AND TRAINING PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT AND TECHNOLOGY 
CENTER (NETPDTC).  THESE ITEMS WILL BE SHIPPED SEPARATELY TO COMMANDS WITH MU1 
CANDIDATES.
CANDIDATES SHALL USE HEADSETS SUPPLIED.  PERSONAL HEADSETS ARE NOT AUTHORIZED.

3.  ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
    A.  IN ADDITION TO THE CRITERIA SET-FORTH IN THIS NAVADMIN, CANDIDATES 
MUST MEET ADVANCEMENT ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS AS OUTLINED IN REF B, CHAPTER 
2, PRIOR TO THE REGULARLY SCHEDULED EXAMINATION ADMINISTRATION DATE FOR THEIR 
PAYGRADE.  SAILORS WHO DO NOT MEET ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS ARE INELIGIBLE FOR 
ADVANCEMENT AND SHALL NOT BE ADMINISTERED A CYCLE 212 ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION.
    B.  TERMINAL ELIGIBILITY DATE (TED):  THE TED FOR CYCLE 212
    IS
1 JAN 2012.  TED REQUIREMENTS ARE SHOWN IN REF B, TABLE 2-5, AND SHALL BE USED 
TO ESTABLISH TIR ELIGIBILITY FOR PARTICIPATION IN ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION 
CYCLES.
    C.  HIGH YEAR TENURE (HYT):  PER REF C, ARTICLE 1160-120, CANDIDATES WHO 
REACH HYT ON OR AFTER 1 JAN 2012, OR WHO HAVE RECEIVED A HYT WAIVER TO REMAIN 
ON ACTIVE DUTY ON OR AFTER 1 JAN 2012, ARE ELIGIBLE TO PARTICIPATE IN THE 
ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION.  CANDIDATES WITH A HYT DATE PRIOR TO 1 JAN 2012 ARE 
NOT ELIGIBLE FOR ADVANCEMENT AND SHALL NOT BE ADMINISTERED AN EXAMINATION.
    D.  NON-DESIGNATED SAILORS:  PER REF D, THE FLEET RATING IDENTIFICATION 
ENGINE (FLEET RIDE) TOOL SHALL BE USED TO VALIDATE RATING QUALIFICATIONS AND 
REQUEST RATING ENTRY VIA ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION PARTICIPATION FOR NON-
DESIGNATED SAILORS.  ONLY FLEET RIDE APPLICATIONS WHICH HAVE BEEN SUBMITTED 
AND APPROVED DURING A MAR 2011 - AUG 2011 RACK/STACK ARE VALID FOR THE CYCLE 
212 EXAMINATION.
NON-DESIGNATED SAILORS WHO DO NOT MEET THIS REQUIREMENT ARE NOT ELIGIBLE FOR 
RATING ENTRY VIA CYCLE 212 AND SHALL NOT BE ADMINISTERED AN EXAMINATION.
    E.  ENLISTED NAVY LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT:  REFER TO REF E.
    F.  WARFARE QUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS:  REFER TO REF F.
    G.  PHYSICAL READINESS PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS:  REFER TO REF G.
    H.  TIR WAIVERS:  GRANTING EARLY PROMOTE TIR WAIVERS AND ADMINISTRATION OF 
EXAMINATIONS TO CANDIDATES MUST BE EXECUTED IN ACCORDANCE WITH PARA 216 OF REF 
B.  TIR WAIVER CANDIDATES SHALL BE IDENTIFIED ON THE TRANSMITTAL LETTER.
    I.  SECURITY CLEARANCE:  PER PARA 204 OF REF B, ALL PERSONNEL
    IN
CERTAIN RATINGS MUST, AT A MINIMUM, MAINTAIN CONTINUOUS SECURITY CLEARANCE 
ELIGIBILITY.  A FAVORABLE INVESTIGATION ADJUDICATION MUST BE ISSUED FROM 
DEPARTMENT OF NAVY CENTRAL ADJUDICATION FACILITY
(DONCAF) PRIOR TO THE REGULARLY SCHEDULED EXAMINATION DATE.  AN INTERIM 
SECURITY CLEARANCE IS NOT A FORMAL DETERMINATION BY AN ADJUDICATIVE AUTHORITY 
AND THEREFORE DOES NOT SATISFY THIS REQUIREMENT.  SAILORS IN THE FOLLOWING 
RATINGS WHO DO NOT MEET THIS REQUIREMENT ARE INELIGIBLE FOR ADVANCEMENT AND 
SHALL NOT BE ADMINISTERED AN EXAMINATION:  AC, AE, AG, AO, AT, AWF, AWO, AWR, 
AWS, AWV, AZ, CTI, CTM, CTN, CTR, CTT, EOD, ET, FC, FT, GM, HT, IC, IS, IT, 
ITS, LN, MA, MC, MN, MT, ND, OS, QM, SB, SO, STG, STS, AND YN, AS WELL AS ALL 
NUCLEAR-TRAINED PERSONNEL.
    J.  SCHOOL CANDIDATES:
       (1) SAILORS WHO HAVE REPORTED TO "A" SCHOOL FOR A
       RATING
CONVERSION AND HAVE NOT GRADUATED PRIOR TO THE REGULAR EXAMINATION ARE NOT 
ELIGIBLE TO PARTICIPATE IN THE EXAMINATION.
       (2) SAILORS WHO GRADUATE FROM MANDATORY CLASS "A"
       SCHOOL BEFORE
THE REGULAR EXAMINATION DATE AND ARE QUALIFIED IN ALL RESPECTS ARE ELIGIBLE TO 
COMPETE FOR ADVANCEMENT IN THEIR NEW RATING.
       (3) NON-DESIGNATED SAILORS IN INITIAL ENTRY TRAINING,
       TO
INCLUDE "A" SCHOOL, WHO ENLISTED UNDER AN "A" SCHOOL GUARANTEE PROGRAM ARE 
INELIGIBLE FOR PARTICIPATION IN THE EXAMINATION UNTIL COMPLETION OF CLASS "A" 
SCHOOL.
    K.  BRANCH CLASS (BRCL) AND SPECIAL PROGRAM INDICATOR (SPI)
    CODE:
THE NAVY ACCOUNTING STATUS FOR BRCL 32 SAILORS MUST BE VERIFIED VIA THE 
COMMAND'S ENLISTED DISTRIBUTION VERIFICATION REPORT (EDVR).
PERSONNEL WITH A BLANK SPECIAL PROGRAM INDICATOR (SPI) CODE ARE IN AN INVALID 
STATUS WHICH MAY RESULT IN ASSIGNMENT OF AN INCORRECT ADVANCEMENT COMPETITIVE 
CATEGORY AND THEIR EXAMINATIONS WILL BE INVALIDATED.  COMMANDS MUST CORRECT 
THE ERROR VIA NAVY STANDARD INTEGRATED PERSONNEL SYSTEM (NSIPS) OR THE SAILOR 
MAY SEEK RELIEF BY PETITIONING THE BOARD FOR CORRECTION OF NAVAL RECORDS 
(BCNR).
