RMKS/1.  The Career Recruiter Force (CRF) was established in 1978 to develop 
a cadre of exceptional recruiting managers to provide consistency and 
leadership to the Navy*s recruiting effort. Navy Recruiting Command manages 
the size, qualifications, and career path of the CRF community.

2.  The CRF program is open to personnel who are currently assigned to 
recruiting duty with Navy Enlisted Classification (NEC) codes 9585, 9586 or 
9587 and to personnel who have previously completed a successful tour as 
recruiters and desire to convert to the Navy Counselor (Recruiter) rating.  
Further eligibility criteria and application procedures are listed in 
reference (a).

3.  An administrative board will be held monthly, or as needed, and 
determined by Commander, Navy Recruiting Command and BUPERS 326 to consider 
applicants for conversion to CRF.

4.  Forward all CRF conversion requests to:
    Commander Navy Recruiting Command
    ATTN:  Assistant CRF Program Manager, N11151
    5722 Integrity Drive, Bldg 784
    Millington, TN 38054-5028

Requests may be scanned and sent via encrypted e-mail directly to Mr. Richard 
Thompson at richard.e.thompson2.ctr(at)navy.mil.

5.  All candidates will be notified via official message of their selection 
or non-selection.

6.  Selectees currently on recruiting duty will be converted immediately to 
Navy Counselor (Recruiter) (NEC-2186).  Selectees not on recruiting duty will 
be immediately assigned a temporary NEC 2199.  Fleet personnel selected will 
take their current rating Navy-wide advancement examination until 
successfully graduating from the CRF academy.  Selectees will attend the CRF 
academy in Pensacola, FL in conjunction with permanent change of station 
orders at their current projected rotation date.

7.  Point of contact is NCCM Clemente James, CRF Program Manager, at (901) 
874-9136/DSN 882 or Mr. Richard Thompson, Assistant CRF Program Manager, at 
(901) 874-9292/DSN 882.

8.  This NAVADMIN will remain in effect until superseded or canceled, 
whichever comes first.

9.  Released by Vice Admiral W. F. Moran, N1.//
