RMKS/1.  The Command Advancement Program (CAP) and the Navy
Recruiter Meritorious Advancement Program (NRMAP) are intended to
reward sustained superior performance, providing command triads
further opportunities to advance their top Sailors.  The changes
outlined in this NAVADMIN are based on Fleet feedback and are
intended to strengthen these programs to ensure continued success
and viability.

2.  As part of our performance-based initiative and to better align
the CAP with advancement cycles, the following changes have been
made effective 1 October 2014 for active component (AC) and reserve
component (RC) commands Navy-wide.
    a.  An Annual CAP NAVADMIN will be released listing eligible
command UICs and maximum quotas.  Quota limitations are outlined in
reference (a).
    b.  CAP Validation.
        (1) Commands will forward their CAP certification letter
(copy to ISIC) to Enlisted Career Administration/Enlisted Boards
(PERS-81) with their CAP selects listed in accordance with
reference (a).
        (2)  PERS-81 will review the record(s) of the selected
Sailor(s) and validate the CAP letter.  If there are no
discrepancies PERS-81 will inform the command that the CAP quota(s)
are executable.
        (3)  If discrepancies are discovered, PERS-81 will
invalidate the CAP letter and notify the command of the reason(s)
for invalidation.  Commands would then be able to correct the
discrepancy or identify new CAP candidates(s).
    c.  Effective dates.  The effective date of validated CAP
candidates will be as follows:  Time-in-rate (TIR):  1 July
(of current year) rate-of-rate (DOR):  Date of CO*s signature on
CAP certification letter between 1 July - 30 September
(of current year).  CAPs will not be executed until the CO has
received authorization from PERS-81 (DOR will be retroactive to the
CO*s signature on CAP certification letter, but no earlier than
1 July).  If operational commitments preclude a command from
submitting their CAP certification prior to the deadline, the
command shall forward the CAP certification letter with
justification to their ISIC for endorsement and further processing
through PERS-81.
    d.  Pay, Personnel and Benefits Branch (PERS-2) will be the sole
source to effect validated CAP advancements based on PERS-81*s
validation.
    e.  CAP Sailor*s Navy-wide advancement exam will be invalidated.
    f.  COs may waive up to 12 months of the required TIR for E5
Sailors who have received a promotion recommendation of
*Early Promote* on the most recent observed periodic evaluation.
    g.  All ratings/paygrades (E3-E5) are eligible for CAP.  Future
CAP NAVADMINs may close ratings for CAP consideration due to the
health of the community as directed by the Chief of Naval Personnel
(CNP).

3.  Revised CAP timeline.
    a.  30 September 2014, current CAP policy outlined in
reference (a), ended per reference (c).  In accordance with
reference (c), commands were to submit their 2014 Annual CAP Report
to PERS-81, in accordance with reference (a), NLT 15 October 2014.
Quotas from the current CAP will not carry-over into the new CAP
period.
    b.  1 October 2014, CAP shifted from a calendar year program to
a fiscal year (FY) program.
    c.  The period of observance for FY-15 CAP began 1 October 2014
and ends 30 September 2015.
    d.  The FY-15 CAP season will commence 1 July 2015 and end
30 September 2015.  This is the only time period commands can
request and effect CAP advancements.
    e.  All FY-15 CAP letters must be received by PERS-81 by the
30 September 2015 deadline.  Any letters received after this
deadline will be invalidated and the command will not be able to
advance their Sailors unless delayed reporting is approved in
accordance with paragraph 2c of this message.
