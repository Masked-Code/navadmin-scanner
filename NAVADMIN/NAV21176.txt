AMPN/REF A IS FISCAL YEAR 2022 NATIONAL COMMAND AND SENIOR OFFICER COMMANDER 
OR CAPTAIN NON-COMMAND BILLET SCREENING AND ASSIGNMENT PROCEDURES.// 
 
RMKS/1.  This NAVADMIN announces the convening of the Fiscal Year (FY) 2022 
Navy Reserve National Command and Senior Officer (O5/O6) Non-Command Billet 
Screening and Assignment (APPLY) Board on 18 October 2021.  All Selected 
Reserve officer communities will utilize the APPLY Board process for senior 
officer and command assignments. 
 
2.  Eligible officers desiring a billet assignment are required to complete 
and submit all applicable sections of the APPLY application.  Applications 
must be submitted via the Reserve Forces Manpower Tools (RFMT) website 
available at https://rfmt.private.navyreserve.navy.mil.  Officers are 
encouraged to place the maximum number of billet choices on their dreamsheets 
to maximize the opportunity for an assignment.  In addition, officers are 
highly encouraged to note on their applications their willingness to travel 
for billets not on their dreamsheets.  Doing so will enable the board to 
select the best qualified applicants. 
 
3.  Applications must be finalized with billet preferences made via 
RFMT and submitted no later than (NLT) 2359, 23 August 2021. 
 
4.  All correspondence to the board must be received NLT 2359, 7 October 
2021.  Submission methods are detailed in paragraph 5 below. Late 
correspondence will not be considered by the board. 
 
5.  Board correspondence can be submitted via one of the following 
methods: 
    a.  Encrypted e-mail (preferred) to cscselboard(at)navy.mil. 
Subject line of email should read: 
        FY-22 Navy Reserve National Command and Senior Officer 
        (O5/O6) Non-Command Billet Screening and Assignment (APPLY) 
        Board (#391) ICO (Rank First Name Last Name, Designator). 
    b.  Standard (US Postal Service) mail (if encrypted e-mail is not 
available) to: 
        MyNavy Career Center 
        FY-22 Navy Reserve National Command and Senior Officer 
        (O5/O6) Non-Command Billet Screening and Assignment (APPLY) 
        Board (#391) 
        5720 Integrity Drive 
        Bldg 768, Room E302 
        Millington, TN 38055-9200 
    c.  Overnight/Express Mail requiring signature: 
        MyNavy Career Center 
        FY-22 Navy Reserve National Command and Senior Officer 
        (O5/O6) Non-Command Billet Screening and Assignment (APPLY) 
        Board (#391) 
        5640 Ticonderoga Loop 
        Millington, TN 38055-9200 
 
    d.  Applicants may contact MyNavy Career Center to confirm receipt of 
their packages and ask questions concerning supplemental/additional 
correspondence at 833-330-MNCC/833-330-6622, 901-874-MNCC/901-874-6622 or via 
e-mail at askmncc(at)navy.mil. 
 
6.  A Selected Reserve officer must be qualified per reference (a) to be 
considered for an assignment.  A qualified officer must complete and submit a 
dreamsheet with at least one billet on their application in RFMT to be 
considered by the APPLY Board for a billet. 
 
7.  An APPLY-eligible officer who is not affiliated with the Reserve 
component in a drilling status (e.g. Active Status Pool/Voluntary 
Participation Unit, Recall, Mobilization) is still required to complete and 
submit a dreamsheet to participate in APPLY and to be eligible for a post 
board assignment.  Non-affiliated officers, and any officers experiencing 
difficulty submitting a dreamsheet in RFMT, should contact Commander, Navy 
Reserve Forces Command (COMNAVRESFORCOM) (N122) at 
cnrfc_apply_mailbox(at)navy.mil for assistance. 
 
8.  APPLY Board assignments are assumed to be accepted by selected officers, 
whereby no further action is required.  Officers who desire to decline 
awarded billets must do so within seven days following release of official 
results via the RFMT website. 
 
9.  Point of contact:  All APPLY questions or concerns should be addressed to 
COMNAVRESFORCOM (N122) APPLY team via e-mail at 
cnrfc_apply_mailbox(at)navy.mil. 
 
10.  This NAVADMIN will remain in effect until superseded or canceled, 
whichever occurs first. 
 
11.  Released by Vice Admiral John B. Nowell, Jr, N1.//
