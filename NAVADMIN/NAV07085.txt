RMKS/1.  THIS NAVADMIN ANNOUNCES THE OVERSEAS DUTY AND IA SCREENING 
RESULTS REPORTING ON BOL.  THE BOL APPLICATION FOR CENTRALIZED 
REPORTING AND TRACKING OF OVERSEAS AND IA SCREENING HAS BEEN ENHANCED 
TO STREAMLINE THE REPORTING PROCESS FOR TRANSFERRING COMMANDS AND TO 
PROVIDE GAINING COMMANDS WITH REAL TIME SCREENING STATUS REPORTS.
2.  REPORTING VIA BOL WILL FEED INTO THE ASSIGNMENT INFORMATION 
DISTRIBUTION SYSTEMS WITHOUT ANY MANUAL INTERVENTION.  UNSUITABLE 
REPORTS WILL BE FORWARDED TO DETAILERS FOR FURTHER ACTION, AND GAINING 
COMMANDS WILL BE ABLE TO REVIEW SCREENING STATUS FOR THEIR PROSPECTIVE 
GAINS.
3.  THE BOL APPLICATION LABELED  OVERSEAS SCREENING  WILL REPLACE THE 
CURRENT MESSAGE REPORTING SYSTEM.  COMMAND REPRESENTATIVES WITH COMMAND 
CAREER COUNSELOR OR ABOVE ACCESS WILL BE ABLE TO ACCESS THE APPLICATION 
AND PROVIDE INPUTS.  THE APPLICATION WILL BE FORMATTED WITH PULL DOWN 
AND FILL IN MENUS.  THE CURRENT MESSAGE REPORTING PROCESS WILL REMAIN 
IN PLACE FOR UNITS WHO EXPERIENCE CONNECTIVITY PROBLEMS.
4.  COMMANDS WILL PROVIDE PSD S/PERSONNEL OFFICE WITH THE NAVPERS
1300/16
SIGNED BY THE MTF AND TRANSFERRING COMMANDING OFFICER, THE NAVAL 
MESSAGE WILL NOT BE REQUIRED.
5.  IA REPORTING:  COMMANDS NOMINATING CANDIDATES FOR IA ASSIGNMENTS 
MUST ENSURE THE MEDICAL SUITABILITY SCREENING IS CONDUCTED FOR BOTH 
PRIMARY AND ALTERNATE NOMINEES IAW THE ECRC IA CHECKLIST AND DA FORM 
7425 PROVIDED ON NKO.
WHEN SCREENING IS COMPLETE, ENTER THE RESULTS IN BOL IN THE  
OVERSEAS/IA SCREENING  APPLICATION UNDER THE  AUGMENTATION SCREENING  
INPUT TAB.
6.  REVISIONS TO MILPERSMAN 1300-300 THROUGH 316 AND OPNAVINST 1300.14D 
WILL REFLECT THESE CHANGES.
