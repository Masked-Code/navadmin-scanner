RMKS/1.  Per ref (a), this NAVADMIN announces navy drug screening labs (NDSL) 
will add synthetic cannabinoids (SC), also referred to as "spice", "K-2", or 
"herbal incense", to the standard testing panel for urinalysis samples 
submitted for testing.  Samples submitted to NDSLS for regular urinalysis 
testing will be tested for SC.  Any Sailor whose urinalysis sample is 
determined to be positive for SC may be considered in violation of the 
Uniform Code of Military Justice, Article 112A For Drug Misuse/Abuse and 
subject to administrative and disciplinary actions.  Those Sailors found to 
be drug dependent will be offered treatment prior to their separation, if 
eligible.
 
2.  Probable cause and command-directed premise code testing, as defined in 
ref (b), still require authorization from Navy Alcohol and Drug Abuse 
Prevention Office (OPNAV N170A) before submitting urinalysis samples to Armed 
Forces Medical Examiner Services. Guidance for authorization testing is 
available in the Synthetic Drug Urinalysis Operating Guide at 
www.nadap.navy.mil.
 
3.  Point of contact is Ms. Lanorfeia Parker, Navy Alcohol and Drug Abuse 
Prevention Office, at 901-874-4249/DSN 882-4249, 901-481-6295, OR via e-mail 
at lanorfeia.parker(at)navy.mil.
