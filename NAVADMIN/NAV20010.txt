RMKS/1.  This order to account applies to all Navy Active Duty, Reservists, 
DON Civilian Employees, NAF Employees, NEX Employees, OCONUS Contractors, and 
Applicable Dependents.  Echelon II Commanders and all Regional Commanders 
shall ensure widest dissemination to all subordinate and tenant commands and 
detachments for action as soon as safety allows.

2.  The following geographic area of interest (GAOI) has been established for 
Taal volcano eruption: Philippines

3.  All commands are expected to:
    a.  Muster and account for all Navy Active Duty, Reserve, DON Civilian
    Employees, NAF Employees, NEX Employees, and applicable dependents in the 
GAOI as soon as safety allows.  All Commanding Officer representatives in the 
affected GAOI will Receive notification from NFAAS to muster themselves and 
dependents in Navy Family Accountability and Assessment System (NFAAS) as 
soon as safety allows.  Commanding Officers (COs) and/or CORs will ensure 
widest dissemination of requirement for accurate and timely accountability 
via NFAAS.
COs and/or CORs are to determine accurate status and whereabouts of their 
personnel and muster and account for them at current residence, work, 
telework, displaced location, or not in the GAOI with specified circumstance 
(deployed, TDY/TAD/IA, on leave, separated/retired, transferred, UA/Deserter, 
other).  In doing so, an accurate accounting of all personnel in all 
accounting categories may be quickly determined.
    b.  COs and/or CORs utilize the NFAAS support site at 
https://navyfamily.navy.noclick_mil to account for each individual required 
to be reported per references a and b.
    c.  If NFAAS is not available, commands/members are to report the status 
of Personnel Accountability (PA) to the Region NFAAS Coordinator as listed in 
Paragraph 6.
    d.  Do not delay reporting on personnel until all information is 
complete. Report and account mustering information when obtained including 
the posting of amplifying data in the notes area on the individual's 
accounting line.

4.  All CORs/Sponsors are expected to:
    a.  Account for themselves and dependents in NFAAS as soon as safety 
allows and notification via https://navyfamily.navy.noclick_mil.
    b.  If unable to access, NFAAS sponsors should contact their COR, Command 
Mustering Officer, or supervisor to check in (Muster).
    c.  Sponsors will provide command representatives with current residence, 
displaced location, or not in the GAOI with specified circumstance (deployed, 
TDY/TAD/IA, on leave, separated/retired, transferred, UA/Deserter, other).
    d.  Family members can log on to NFAAS at 
https://navyfamily.navy.noclick_mil and check in
(Muster) on the Navy family members site.  If unable to log on to NFAAS and 
for technical support, call the NFAAS help desk at (866) 946-9183 (For 
technical support only).
    e.  If unsuccessful, call the Emergency Coordination Center (ECC):  (877) 
414-5358; ECC TDD:  (866) 297-1971.

5.  Navy families who were severely impacted are strongly encouraged to log 
into NFAAS to update contact information and complete a Needs Assessment Form 
at their earliest convenience.
For further assistance after a Needs Assessment Form has been submitted, call 
Navy Personnel Command Emergency Coordination Center (NPC ECC) at (877) 414-
5358 or e-mail npc.cat.captain(AT)navy.mil.

6.  If unable to muster via NFAAS, your COR, your mustering POC, or your 
chain of command, contact the below POCs (location/phone/email):
    a.  NPC ECC/877-414-5358/npc.cat.captain(AT)navy.mil.
    b.  CNIC BWC/202-433-0226/cnic.n36.bwc(AT)navy.mil.
    c.  COMPACFLT BWC/808-474-5452/cpfbwc(AT)navy.mil.
    d.  NFAAS Program Managers/202-433-4321/isa.grace(AT)navy.mil or 202-433-
0763/meredith.henkelman(AT)navy.mil.
