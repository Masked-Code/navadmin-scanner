RMKS/1.  The Chief of Naval Operations is pleased to congratulate the 2015 
Copernicus Award winners.  The Copernicus Award recognizes individual 
contributions to Naval Warfare in the disciplines of Command, Control, 
Communications, Computers, and Intelligence (C4I), Information Systems or 
Information Warfare (IW).  The Armed Forces Communications and Electronics 
Association (AFCEA) and the U.S. Naval Institute (USNI) have selected the 
following Copernicus Award winners for 2015: 

    a.  LT Kirsten M. Ambors-Casey, U.S. Coast Guard Base New Orleans
    b.  Mr. Joseph M. Andrew, Naval Special Warfare Command
    c.  LCDR Daniel W. Berger, U.S. Naval Forces Central Command
    d.  LT Jeremy E. Blanchette, USS PREBLE (DDG 88)
    e.  Ms. Mary A. Brereton, Naval Undersea Warfare Center Division Newport
    f.  LCDR Terrell A. Burnett, U.S. Naval Computer and Telecommunications 
Station Bahrain
    g.  Capt Richard S. Clay, U.S. Marine Corps Forces Europe and Africa
    h.  LTJG John J. Dannemiller, Carrier Airborne Early Warning Squadron ONE 
TWO FIVE

2.  AFCEA and USNI will send letters of congratulations to all Copernicus 
Award winners, inviting them, their spouses or a guest, and their Commanding

AFCEA/USNI West conference in San Diego, CA on 17 February 2016.
