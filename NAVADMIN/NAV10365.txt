SUBJ/MARCH 2011 (CYCLE 211) NAVY-WIDE PETTY OFFICER (E4-E6) ADVANCEMENT 
EXAMINATIONS FOR USN, USNR (ACTIVE), FULL TIME SUPPORT, AND CANVASSER 
RECRUITER SAILORS//

RMKS/1.  THIS NAVADMIN ANNOUNCES THE MARCH 2011 (CYCLE 211) NAVY- WIDE 
ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION FOR ADVANCEMENT IN RATE OF USN/USNR (ACTIVE), 
FULL TIME SUPPORT (FTS) TO INCLUDE ACTIVE GUARD AND RESERVE (AGR), AND 
CANVASSER RECRUITER (CANREC) SAILORS.  THE EXAMINATION DATES ARE:
    E6 - 3 MARCH 2011
    E5 - 10 MARCH 2011
    E4 - 17 MARCH 2011
 
    A.  REFER TO REF A REGARDING ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION OPTIONS FOR 
SAILORS WHO ARE PENDING DEPLOYMENT TO, OR ARE DEPLOYED AND OPERATING IN 
IRAQ, AFGHANISTAN, OR THE HORN OF AFRICA.
 
    B.  DO NOT ADMINISTER ANY CYCLE 211 EXAMINATIONS PRIOR TO THE 
SCHEDULED EXAMINATION DATE UNLESS PROVIDED PER REF A OR REF B, OR 
SPECIFICALLY AUTHORIZED BY NAVY PERSONNEL COMMAND PERS-812.
 
    C.  MUSICIAN (MU) SECOND CLASS PETTY OFFICERS WILL TAKE THE REGULAR 
MU1 PAPER EXAMINATION ON 3 MARCH 2011 AND WILL USE A DIGITAL MEDIA 
PLAYER PROVIDED BY NAVY EDUCATION AND TRAINING PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT 
AND TECHNOLOGY CENTER (NETPDTC).  THESE ITEMS WILL BE SHIPPED SEPARATELY 
TO COMMANDS WITH MU1 CANDIDATES.  CANDIDATES SHALL USE HEADSET SUPPLIED.  
PERSONAL HEADSETS ARE NOT AUTHORIZED.

3.  ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
 
    A.  CANDIDATES MUST MEET THE ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS OUTLINED IN 
REF B, CHAPTER 2, PRIOR TO EXAMINATION ADMINISTRATION DATE FOR THEIR PAY 
GRADE. REFER TO PARAS 2.F, 2.G, AND 2.H OF THIS NAVADMIN FOR EXAMINATION 
ADMINISTRATION DATES.
 
    B.  TERMINAL ELIGIBILITY DATE (TED):  THE TED FOR CYCLE 211 IS 1 
JULY
2011.  THIS DATE SHALL BE USED TO COMPUTE AND DETERMINE TIR ELIGIBILITY 
IN ACCORDANCE WITH REF B, TABLE 2-2.
 
    C.  HIGH YEAR TENURE (HYT):  IN ACCORDANCE WITH REF C, ARTICLE 1160-
120, CANDIDATES WHO REACH HYT ON OR AFTER 1 JULY 2011, OR WHO HAVE 
RECEIVED A HYT WAIVER TO REMAIN ON ACTIVE DUTY ON OR AFTER 1 JULY 2011, 
ARE ELIGIBLE TO PARTICIPATE IN THE ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION.  CANDIDATES 
WITH A HYT DATE PRIOR TO 1 JULY 2011 ARE NOT ELIGIBLE TO COMPETE FOR 
ADVANCEMENT AND SHALL NOT BE ADMINISTERED AN EXAMINATION.
 
    D.  NON-DESIGNATED SAILORS:  COMMANDS ARE REQUIRED TO USE THE FLEET 
RATING IDENTIFICATION ENGINE (FLEET RIDE) TOOL TO VALIDATE RATING 
QUALIFICATIONS AND REQUEST RATING ENTRY VIA ADVANCEMENT EXAMINATION 
PARTICIPATION FOR NON-DESIGNATED SAILORS.  COMPLETE FLEET RIDE 
APPLICATIONS IN ACCORDANCE WITH REF D.
 
    E.  ENLISTED NAVY LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT:  REFER TO REF E.
    F.  WARFARE QUALIFICATION REQUIREMENTS:  REFER TO REF F.
    G.  PHYSICAL READINESS PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS:  REFER TO REF G.
 
    H.  TIR WAIVERS:  TIR WAIVERS AND EXAMINATION ADMINISTRATION OF 
EARLY
PROMOTE CANDIDATES MUST BE IN ACCORDANCE WITH REF A AND REF B, PARA 
216.F.
CANDIDATES WHO HAVE BEEN GRANTED TIR WAIVERS MUST BE IDENTIFIED DURING 
EXAMINATION ORDERING AND ON THE TRANSMITTAL LETTER TO NETPDTC (N321).
 
    I.  SECURITY CLEARANCE:  PER REF B, PARA 204, PERSONNEL IN
    THE FOLLOWING RATINGS MUST BE ELIGIBLE FOR A CLEARANCE WITH A 
FAVORABLE INVESTIGATION ADJUDICATED FROM DONCAF PRIOR TO REGULAR 
SCHEDULED EXAMINATION DATE: AC, AE, AG, AO, AT, AWF, AWO, AWR, AWS, AWV, 
AZ, CT, EOD, ET, FC, FT, GM, HT, IC, IS, IT, LN, MA, MC, MN, MT, ND, OS, 
QM, SB, SO, STG, STS, AND YN, AS WELL AS ALL NUCLEAR TRAINED PERSONNEL.
 
    J. "A" SCHOOL CANDIDATES:
        (1) SAILORS WHO HAVE REPORTED TO "A" SCHOOL FOR A RATING 
CONVERSION AND HAVE NOT GRADUATED PRIOR TO THE REGULAR EXAMINATION ARE 
NOT ELIGIBLE TO PARTICIPATE IN THE EXAMINATION.
        (2) SAILORS WHO GRADUATE FROM MANDATORY CLASS "A" SCHOOL BEFORE 
THE REGULAR EXAMINATION DATE AND ARE QUALIFIED IN ALL RESPECTS ARE 
ELIGIBLE TO COMPETE FOR ADVANCEMENT IN THEIR NEW RATING.
        (3) NON-DESIGNATED SAILORS IN INITIAL ENTRY TRAINING, TO INCLUDE 
"A" SCHOOL, WHO ENLISTED UNDER AN "A" SCHOOL GUARANTEE PROGRAM ARE 
INELIGIBLE FOR PARTICIPATION IN THE EXAMINATION UNTIL COMPLETION OF 
CLASS "A" SCHOOL .
 
    K.  BRANCH CLASS (BRCL) AND SPECIAL PROGRAM INDICATOR (SPI) CODE:  
THE
NAVY ACCOUNTING STATUS FOR BRCL 32 SAILORS MUST BE VERIFIED VIA THE 
COMMAND'S ENLISTED DISTRIBUTION VERIFICATION REPORT (EDVR).  PERSONNEL 
WITH A BLANK SPI CODE ARE IN AN INVALID STATUS WHICH MAY RESULT IN 
ASSIGNMENT OF AN INCORRECT ADVANCEMENT COMPETITIVE CATEGORY AND THEIR 
EXAMINATIONS WILL BE INVALIDATED. COMMANDS MUST CORRECT THE ERROR VIA 
NAVY STANDARD INTEGRATED PERSONNEL SYSTEM (NSIPS) OR THE SAILOR MAY SEEK 
RELIEF BY PETITIONING THE BOARD FOR CORRECTION OF NAVAL RECORDS (BCNR).

5.  GENERAL EXAMINATION INFORMATION:
 
    A.  ENSURE ALL CANDIDATES, ESOS, PROCTORS, AND ADMINISTRATORS HAVE 
REQUIRED CLEARANCE AND ACCESS PRIOR TO EXAMINATION DAY.
 
    B.  EDUCATION POINTS:  IN ACCORDANCE WITH REF I, EDUCATION POINTS 
WILL BE AWARDED FOR THE HIGHEST ACCREDITED DEGREE HELD; (TWO POINTS FOR 
AN ASSOCIATES DEGREE OR FOUR POINTS FOR A BACCALAUREATE DEGREE OR 
ABOVE).  TO ALLOW SUFFICIENT TIME FOR THE NAVY COLLEGE CENTER TO PROCESS 
ALL DOCUMENTS, TRANSCRIPTS MUST BE RECEIVED FROM ADVANCEMENT CANDIDATES 
BY 1 MARCH 2011.  THE NAVY COLLEGE CENTER WILL VALIDATE TRANSCRIPTS AND 
ENTER THE EDUCATION DATA INTO THE NAVY COLLEGE MANAGEMENT INFORMATION 
SYSTEM (NCMIS) TO UPDATE AND TO BE USED TO CALCULATE FMS.
 
    C.  FOR RATINGS WITH MULTIPLE EXAMINATIONS, IT IS IMPERATIVE ESO'S 
ENSURE PROPER EXAMINATION IDENTIFICATION IS USED DURING EXAMINATION 
ORDERING AND ANSWER SHEET VERIFICATION (I.E., NCR/NCC, ATI/ATO, 
ETR/ETV/ETN, ETC.)  INCORRECT EXAMINATION IDENTIFICATION WILL CAUSE 
EXAMINATIONS TO BE INCORRECTLY GRADED.
