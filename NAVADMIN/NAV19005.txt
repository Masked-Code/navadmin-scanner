SUBJ/NOTICE OF CONVENING FY-20 ACTIVE-DUTY NAVY REAR ADMIRAL (LOWER HALF)
FOREIGN EXPERTISE (FE1) PROMOTION SELECTION BOARD//

RMKS/1.  The FY-20 Promotion Selection Board to recommend officers on the 
Active-Duty List serving in the grade of captain for promotion to the grade 
of rear admiral (lower half) serving in the Unrestricted Line, Foreign Area 
Officer, Oceanography, Cryptologic Warfare, Information Professional and 
Intelligence communities, who hold the FE1 additional qualification 
designation (AQD) and not on a promotion list to Rear Admiral (lower half) 
will be convened as follows:
20 FEB 19 -  Foreign Expertise (AQD FE1) Rear Admiral (lower half)

2.  The following lists the name, Active-Duty List number, and date of rank 
of the senior and junior officers initially eligible for consideration for 
promotion in the competitive category listed as of the date of this NAVADMIN.

                       Rear Admiral (lower half)
--- Foreign Expertise Community (11XX/13XX/171X/180X/181X/182X/183X) ---
Sr Initial Eligible  Cribbs, D. E.              012558-00  01 JAN 13
Jr Initial Eligible  Tully, J. M.               016293-50  01 OCT 16

3.  Officers selected by the Foreign Expertise selection board will be 
assigned to the Foreign Expertise community upon promotion to rear admiral 
(lower half) and will no longer compete for promotion in their previous 
communities.

4.  Only eligible officers may communicate directly with a selection board.  
Correspondence must arrive NLT 2359, 9 Feb 19, 10 calendar days before the 
convening date of the board and shall be addressed to:
President FY-20 Active O-7, Foreign Expertise Community Promotion Selection 
Board, Board #XXX (board number is a required field and may be found on the 
FY-20 selection board schedule located at https://www.public.navy.mil/bupers-
npc/boards/selectionboardsupport/Pages/FY20-Board-Schedule.aspx 
5720 Integrity Drive, Millington, TN 38055-6300.
To check on receipt of your package, go to http://www.public.navy.mil/bupers-
npc/boards/activedutyofficer/Pages/default.aspx and enter the customer 
Service center link (check on your letter to the board) or contact the MyNavy 
Career Center at (833)330-MNCC.  Written communication may call to the 
attention of the board any matter concerning himself/herself that the officer 
considers important.
The written communication must be in accordance with MILPERSMAN article 1420-
010 and may include, as enclosures, correspondence from any individual 
concerning the eligible officer.  Correspondence not originated by the 
eligible officer, including endorsements to the officer's letter, and letters 
written on behalf of that officer, must contain a written acknowledgement by 
the eligible officer that he or she desires that such correspondence be 
presented to the board.
Correspondence without such acknowledgment is considered third party 
correspondence and will be returned.  Classified correspondence will not be 
accepted.

5.  The cscselboard(at)navy.mil mailbox account is capable of receiving 
encrypted correspondence IAW DoD policy.  If you are sending your board 
package from a non NMCI network (bumed.mil, usmc.mil, etc), please visit 
https://dod411.gds.disa.mil to download the required mailbox certificate.
To download the "cert" (please note, you must be using MS outlook with a CAC 
reader, including "activclient" software and have internet explorer or 
Netscape 7), go to https://dod411.gds.disa.mil, type cscselboard(at)navy.mil 
in the "e-mail address" field, then click "search." Click the "CSC" link 
under "last name."  Click on the link "download certificate(s) as vcard."  
Click "software certificate for cscselboard(at)navy.mil."  Click the "open" 
button for the file download popup.  The certificate will then open.  Click 
"save and close." The certificate is then saved to the profile and can be 
used to send your encrypted e-mail.

6.  Released by Vice Admiral R. P. Burke, N1.//
