GENTEXT/REMARKS/1.  THIS NAVADMIN ANNOUNCES NECESSARY CHANGES TO
EXISTING SEA/SHORE TOUR LENGTHS (REF A) DUE TO CHANGES IN THE NAVY
BILLET STRUCTURE AND INDIVIDUAL RATING SEA/SHORE BILLET RATIOS.
THESE NEW TOUR LENGTHS ARE DESIGNED TO BETTER ALIGN PROJECTED
PERSONNEL INVENTORIES WITH FUNDED BILLET REQUIREMENTS.  WE HAVE BEEN
MAKING ASSIGNMENTS BASED ON A SSR THAT REFLECTS THE BILLET BASE OF
2001 AND DOES NOT TAKE INTO ACCOUNT THE ALMOST 19.6K ENLISTED SHORE
DUTY BILLETS THAT HAVE BEEN PROGRAMMED FOR ELIMINATION TO DATE.
2.  THE SEA/SHORE ROTATIONS ANNOUNCED IN THIS MESSAGE APPLY TO ALL
ACTIVE DUTY PERSONNEL AND SUPERCEDE ROTATIONS ANNOUNCED IN REF A.
CURRENT FULL-TIME SUPPORT (FTS) SEA/SHORE ROTATION POLICY WILL
REMAIN IN EFFECT UNTIL REALIGNMENT OF THE FTS BILLET STRUCTURE IS
COMPLETE, ANTICIPATED IN MAR 07.
3.  COMNAVPERSCOM WILL ADJUST PROJECTED ROTATION DATES (PRDS) BASED
ON LENGTH OF TOUR REMAINING.  IN ORDER TO MINIMIZE TURBULENCE IN
PERSONAL PLANNING, PRDS OF FEB 07 OR EARLIER WILL NOT BE ADJUSTED.
COMNAVPERSCOM IS AUTHORIZED TO ADJUST A PRD TO REFLECT A SENIOR
PAYGRADE FOR SAILORS WHO ARE ADVANCED IN GRADE DURING THEIR CURRENT
TOUR.  THIS DECISION WILL BE MADE BASED ON OVERALL MANNING IN THE
COMMAND, PROJECTION OF FUTURE ROTATIONS TO FILL TO THE NEW PRD, AND
ENHANCEMENT TO THE CAREER DEVELOPMENT OF THE SAILOR AT THE NEW
PAYGRADE.
4.  WE ARE PREPARING FOR SOME FUNDAMENTAL CHANGES IN HOW WE DO
BUSINESS.  NEW SHIPS AND SQUADRONS COMING ON LINE WILL REQUIRE
DIFFERENT MANNING CONSTRUCTS FROM THOSE IN THE FLEET TODAY.  THE
SEA/SHORE ROTATION CHANGES OUTLINED IN THIS NAVADMIN SHOULD BE
CONSIDERED AN INTERIM STEP AS WE WORK TOWARD NEW MANNING CONSTRUCTS.
 THE SEA/SHORE ROTATION WORKING GROUP, WHICH INCLUDES FLEET
REPRESENTATIVES, IS EXAMINING MANY SOLUTIONS WHICH COULD INCLUDE:
MULTI-CREWED SHIPS, OPERATIONAL/OPERATIONAL SUPPORT DEPLOYMENT
ROTATIONS, AND NEW INCENTIVES/COMPENSATION SYSTEMS.  THEIR OBJECTIVE
IS TO BETTER SUPPORT FLEET AND MISSION REQUIREMENTS AND TO PROVIDE
MORE PREDICTABLE CAREER TRACKS FOR OUR SAILORS.  AN UPDATE ON THEIR
PROGRESS WILL BE PROVIDED WITHIN THE NEXT FEW MONTHS.
