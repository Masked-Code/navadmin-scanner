RMKS/1.  This NAVADMIN announces the convening date for subject selection 
board as 15 January 2019.  The following list indicates the eligible Navy 
captain's name, active-duty list number and date of rank of the senior and 
junior officers on the active duty list in the competitive category of the 
Navy Judge Advocate General's Corps, who are eligible for consideration as of 
the date of this NAVADMIN.
Sr Eligible         Bustamante F.         1136700         1 FEB 09
Jr Eligible         Larrea, M.            1600550         1 SEP 15

2.  If either of the two officers selected has a mandatory retirement date 
between 1 March 2019 and 31 July 2022, the board shall reconvene as a 
continuation selection board to consider the
officer(s) for continuation on active duty.

3.  Only eligible officers may communicate directly with a selection board.  
Correspondence must arrive no later than 10 days before the convening date of 
the board and shall be addressed to:  President,
FY-19 Assistant Judge Advocate General of the Navy Assistant Judge Advocate 
General (Civil Law) or Assistant Judge Advocate General (Operations and 
Management) Selection Board 109.  To check on receipt of your package, go to 
http://www.public.navy.mil/bupers-
npc/boards/activedutyofficer/Pages/default.aspx and enter the customer 
service center link (check on your letter to the board) or contact the 
Customer Service Center at (866) 827-5672.  Written communication may call to 
the attention of the board any matter concerning himself/herself that the 
officer considers important.
The written communication must be in accordance with reference (a) and may 
include, as enclosures, correspondence from any individual concerning the 
eligible officer.  If desired, the eligible officer may express a preference 
to be detailed to either Assistant Judge Advocate General (Civil Law) or 
Assistant Judge Advocate General (Operations and Management) in 
correspondence to the board.
Correspondence not originated by the eligible officer, including endorsements 
to the officer's letter and letters written on behalf of that officer, must 
contain a written acknowledgement by the eligible officer that he or she 
desires that such correspondence be presented to the board.  Correspondence 
without such acknowledgment is considered third party correspondence and will 
be returned.
Classified correspondence will not be accepted.

4.  The CSCSELBOARD(AT)navy.mil mailbox account is capable of receiving 
encrypted correspondence in accordance with DoD policy.
If you are sending your board package from a non-NMCI network (e.g., 
BUMED.mil, USMC.mil, etc.), please visit https://dod411.gds.disa.mil to 
download the required mailbox Certificate.  To download the "cert" (please 
note, you MUST be using MS Outlook with a CAC reader, including "ActivClient" 
software and have Internet Explorer or Netscape 7), go to 
https://dod411.gds.disa.mil, type CSCSELBOARD(AT)navy.mil in the "e-mail 
address" field, then click "Search."  Click the "CSC" link under "Last Name."  
Click on the link "Download Certificate(s) as vCard.  Click "Software 
Certificate for CSCSELBOARD(AT)navy.mil.  Click the "Open" button for the 
file download popup.  The certificate will then open.  Click "Save and 
Close."  The certificate is then saved to the profile and can be used to send 
your encrypted e-mail.

5.  Eligible officers not wanting to be considered by this board may submit a 
written request to the board president indicating the desire that his or her 
record not be considered.  Correspondence must arrive no later than 10 days 
before the convening date of the board and shall be addressed to:  President, 
FY-19 Assistant Judge Advocate General of the Navy, Assistant Judge Advocate 
General (Civil Law) or Assistant Judge Advocate General (Operations and
Management) Selection Board 109.  Officers submitting requests not to be 
considered will not be eligible and their records will not be presented to 
the board for consideration.

6.  The following officers are not eligible and their records will not be 
presented to the board for consideration:  any officer who has an approved 
voluntary separation/retirement request, submitted by the officer and not 
required by policy, no later than 10 days before the convening date of the 
board or any officer who was recommended by a previous selection board for 
appointment as an Assistant Judge Advocate General of the Navy, Legal Counsel 
to the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff or Deputy Judge Advocate General 
of the Navy.

7.  Released by Vice Admiral R. P. Burke, N1.//
